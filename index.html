Onyx Control

Privacy Policy — Last updated: March 2026

TL;DR: We don't collect your data. We don't even have a server to put it on. Your couch, your remote, your business.

Onyx Control respects your privacy. This policy explains how the app handles your data — spoiler: it mostly doesn't.

1. Data Collection

Onyx Control does NOT collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. No analytics, no tracking pixels, no "anonymous usage data" that somehow knows your favorite show. The app operates entirely on your local network.

2. Local Storage

The app stores a small amount of data locally on your device — and all of it is encrypted using AES-256 via the Android Keystore system:

  • Pairing credentials — so you don't have to re-pair every time (you're welcome)
  • App preferences — display order, favorites, language setting
  • Cached icon URLs — fetched from Apple's iTunes API to make things look pretty

This data never leaves your device. It lives there, encrypted, minding its own business.

3. Network Communication

The app talks to exactly two things:

  • Your Apple TV — over your local Wi-Fi, using the encrypted Apple Companion Protocol. This is the whole point of the app.
  • Apple's iTunes Search API (itunes.apple.com) — to fetch app icons. No personal or identifying information is sent. We literally just ask "hey, what does Netflix's icon look like?" and that's it.

That's the full list. No cloud servers, no Firebase, no surprise phone-homes.

4. Third-Party Services

Zero analytics. Zero ads. Zero tracking. The only external service is Apple's public iTunes Search API for app artwork.

5. Permissions

Here's why the app asks for what it asks for:

  • Network & Wi-Fi: To find and talk to your Apple TV. Kind of essential for a remote control app.
  • Foreground service: So the connection doesn't drop when you switch apps to check a text.
  • Notifications: To show what's currently playing. Completely optional.

6. Changes

This policy may be updated if we ever add features that affect data handling. Changes will be reflected in the app's store listing. But honestly, our philosophy is simple: your data stays yours.
